Reflection

Despite encountering a few minor challenges during development, the website has largely functioned as intended. I thoroughly enjoyed the process of creating it, as it helped me comprehend each concept covered in the module, and everything began to make more sense as it came together. Initially, I believed that building the website's framework first and adding the design and style later was the best strategy. Since I already had an idea of how I wanted it to look, constructing the framework was relatively simple. After adding the necessary components, I proceeded to add the navigation bar and then the content. Although it took some time to structure the content, I eventually succeeded, especially in the About section, where I wanted to highlight my personal characteristics while maintaining a professional tone and ensuring that I covered the essentials.
The few difficulties I encountered mainly involved aligning, styling, and adhering to the style guide. I had to do some research on W3schools to figure out what I could do and how to make it work to solve the issues. Having worked on the online shop during my internship at Emcar Ltd, I found it much easier to create the web portfolio, but the content to add was extensive. Working on the back end made it much clearer how everything worked, and I was able to thoroughly review each error and make sense of it. I also tested each section to ensure it functioned as intended.
I realized that it took longer than I anticipated to create the portfolio since it involved many aspects, and I need to improve my time management skills. I found myself rushing to cover all parts of the portfolio and the lessons learned in the module, and time went by quickly. It also took me a while to figure out how to use Github and add all the projects while ensuring that everything worked and I had the correct files.
